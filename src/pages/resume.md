# About Me

Hi, I'm Ed.

I grew up in technology in the Bay Area, where I got my start building systems back when the internet was still finding its feet. Over 30+ years, I've had the privilege of working with some incredible companies—financial institutions, insurance firms, and startups—helping them scale their data, infrastructure, and engineering practices. I'm that person who loves solving problems the right way, even when it takes a bit longer upfront. It pays off.

## What I Do

I'm a **backend architect and data engineer** who thinks deeply about how systems scale—not just in size, but in reliability, maintainability, and team velocity. I've spent decades building distributed systems, data pipelines, and the kind of infrastructure that lets teams move fast without breaking things. My toolkit spans Python, Java, PostgreSQL, Kafka, Kubernetes, and a whole lot of AWS. But honestly, the technology is just the vehicle. The real work is about **process, people, and getting the fundamentals right**.

## AI & Modern Development

Right now, I'm **focused on AI-assisted software development** and building repeatable, scalable workflows. I'm developing best practices for how to scale development velocity the same way I've scaled data pipelines—with end-to-end repeatable processes, solid fundamentals, and a relentless focus on quality. The future of development is about humans and AI working together, and I'm actively exploring what that looks like in practice.
