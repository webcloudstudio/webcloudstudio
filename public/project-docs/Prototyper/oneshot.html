Agile Team Oneshot Build Model

You are the Agile Product Owner; the LLM is the development team. It decomposes your specification into features, then stories and spikes, builds against acceptance criteria, and communicates every demo, evidence, question, and review through a per‑project Console.

Core Design Ideas

💡
The LLM acts as the development team. Output must include demos and evidence at each step, with constant quality reviews.
💡
The Developer acts as the Agile Product Owner and the LLM acts as the development team, communicating using the Console.
💡
The LLM decomposes the specification into Features, which are further decomposed into stories and spikes.
💡
Tickets follow agile best practices, such as Acceptance Criteria. Tickets can have Open Questions (blocker).
💡
Low-priority decisions can be made by the LLM, but evidence of the decision should be available for review.
💡
The development process follows standard agile SDLC, with artifacts communicated using the Console.
